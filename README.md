# Atlas of Vernacular Architecture

An interactive atlas for studying vernacular architecture as climate-adaptation knowledge.

The atlas maps buildings, spatial strategies, body-scale shelters, and architectural elements that respond to climate pressures such as heat, cold, humidity, wind, rainfall, solar exposure, glare, and diurnal variability.

The project is developed at the Chair of Building Technology and Climate Responsive Design, Technical University of Munich.

## Editors

Bilge Kobas and Thomas Auer

## Current status

This repository contains an early research prototype. Case entries are being expanded from studio research, atlas editorial work, and external sources.

## Data and method

The atlas combines:

- curated architectural case studies
- student research booklets
- editorial review and supplementary case completion
- climate data from ERA5/Open-Meteo
- future climate projections based on SSP5-8.5
- present-day climate analogue matching
- an editorial taxonomy of climate pressures, mechanisms, strategy families, regional variants, and architectural/body-scale elements

## Source categories

The atlas currently uses the following source categories:

- **TUM Studio** вЂ” entries produced through TUM teaching.
- **TUM Studio + Atlas Editorial Supplement** вЂ” entries first identified through studio work and completed through editorial review.
- **Atlas Curated** вЂ” entries researched and curated directly by the editorial team.
- **Partner Studio** вЂ” entries produced by collaborating universities or teaching partners.
- **External Research** вЂ” entries based on published research or expert submissions.
- **Field / Community Submission** вЂ” entries grounded in fieldwork, local knowledge, or community documentation.
