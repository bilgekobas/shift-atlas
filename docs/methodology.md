# Methodology

This atlas treats vernacular architecture as climate-adaptation knowledge.

Rather than cataloguing forms only as heritage objects, the atlas interprets them through:

- the environmental pressures they respond to
- the physical mechanisms they use
- the transferable strategy families they represent
- the regional variants through which they become culturally and materially specific
- their limits, risks, and transferability

## Analytical sequence

```text
Climate pressure
↓
Architectural/body-scale response
↓
Microclimatic mechanism
↓
Transferability and limits
```

## Editorial principle

A case should not be added only because it is visually interesting. It should show a legible relationship between climate pressure and spatial, material, operational, or body-scale response.
