# Atlas Taxonomy

The atlas uses a layered taxonomy to distinguish climate problems, physical mechanisms, transferable design logics, and local architectural variants.

## Core hierarchy

```text
Condition → Mechanism → Strategy Family → Regional Variant / Case → Element
```

## Condition

The environmental pressure or climatic problem.

Examples:

- Heat stress
- Cold stress
- Solar exposure
- Glare
- Wind exposure
- Humidity
- Monsoon rain
- Dryness
- Diurnal variability
- Resource scarcity

## Mechanism

The physical or environmental process by which a case modifies exposure or microclimate.

Examples:

- Shade
- Ventilate
- Buffer
- Insulate
- Evaporate
- Reflect
- Store heat
- Reduce exposure
- Enable seasonal use

## Strategy Family

The transferable design logic.

Examples:

- Perforated screen
- Translucent screen
- Operable shutter
- Textile canopy
- Arcade / portico
- Vegetated pergola
- Deep roof edge
- Shaded threshold
- Thermal mass courtyard
- Portable shelter
- Wearable climate shelter

## Regional Variant / Case

The mappable, culturally and geographically specific expression.

Examples:

- Mashrabiya — Cairo / MENA
- Jali — Rajasthan / India
- Engawa — Japan
- Thinnai — Tamil Nadu / India
- Gallariji — Malta
- Kepenek — Central Anatolia, Turkey
- Khasi Knup — Meghalaya

## Element

The architectural or body-scale component through which the strategy operates.

Examples:

- Window
- Façade
- Balcony
- Roof edge
- Courtyard
- Threshold
- Textile layer
- Garment
- Portable shelter
